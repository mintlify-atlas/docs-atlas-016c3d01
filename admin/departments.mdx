**Departamentos**, found under **Gestion del Sistema** in the sidebar, is where you organize employees into logical work units. Every employee must belong to a department, and managers can only see employees in their own department. This page explains the department data model, the pre-seeded departments included with the application, and how to add new departments.

## Navigate to Departments

Go to `/admin/departments` in the admin panel, or click **Departamentos** in the left sidebar under **Gestion del Sistema**. The table lists all departments sorted alphabetically by name.

## Department fields

The department form has a single field.

| Field | Label | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| `name` | name | Required. Up to 50 characters. The department name shown throughout the panel. |

The database also stores an optional `employee_id` (used internally to link a manager to the department) and a `roles_id` field, but these are not exposed in the create or edit form — they are managed through the employee and user assignment process.

<Note>
Departments support soft deletes. Deleting a department archives it without permanently removing it from the database, preserving the historical relationship to employees.
</Note>

## Add a new department

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the create form">
    Navigate to `/admin/departments` and click **Nuevo Departamneto** in the top-right corner.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter the department name">
    Type the department name in the **name** field. The name must be filled in — it is the only required field.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save">
    Click **Create**. The new department appears in the alphabetically sorted table and becomes immediately available in the **Departamento** dropdown on the employee form.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Edit a department

Click the edit icon on any department row to open the edit form. Change the name and click **Save**. The updated name propagates automatically to all related employee records and dropdowns throughout the panel.

## Department manager display

The departments table shows a **Jefe Departamento** (department head) column. This column resolves to the full name of any employee in that department whose linked user account has the `manager` role. If no employee in the department has the manager role, the column is empty.

This column is read-only in the departments table — you assign a manager by giving the appropriate user account the `manager` role and ensuring their employee record points to the correct department.

## Relationship to employees

Each employee record stores a `department_id` that references the department they belong to. The relationship is many-to-one: many employees can belong to one department, and one department can have many employees.

When you archive a department, existing employees keep their `department_id` reference. Assign employees to a different active department before archiving if you want to avoid orphaned references.
